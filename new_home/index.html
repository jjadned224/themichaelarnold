About Michael

Let me introduce myself, I am Michael Arnold, a Lean Six Sigma Master Black Belt, which means I solve problems using statistics.

Over 30 years ago, I discovered I could not be an astronaut because I was three inches too tall. I realized to reach space, I would need to become an engineer and build taller spaceships. Since, then if someone says, “That's the way we have always done it.” It is on! We need to solve the problem and change it for the better.

I still have not reached space, but I did learn how to solve problems others said, “could not be solved.” I know how it feels to be frustrated by red tape holding me back from overcoming my biggest problems. This is why I speak and teach manufacturing leaders how to identify and resolve their biggest problems accomplishing their goals, both organizational and personal.

My goal is to help you build a successful problem-solving organization allowing you to improve your team's performance, and maybe even make it to your kid's game.

When I am not speaking or training you can contact me at michael@themichaelarnold.com. I am always looking forward to connecting with and supporting your next event, allow me to help you build your taller spaceship!



























Michael's Talks

Adaptive Leadership for Managers: The Cure for Command-and-Control Leadership

Command and control are lazy leadership, which is so last century. Learn the cure for lazy leadership. Improving as a leader requires learning better leadership soft skills. Since teams are an integral part of any business.

It is not enough to have a group of people working on a project; people must connect and balance each other's strengths. Adaptive Leadership builds on basic team and self-management strategies to help managers and business leaders develop their skills and build a team to tackle any situation.

Successful Storytelling for Engineers: Using Stories to Influence with Data

We are told to let data speak for itself, but this often leads to questions that cannot be answered. This confusion leads to others not trusting the data or the insights found. Using a story to make a connection and then sharing data in a friendly environment makes for a successful persuasion strategy to drive necessary changes.

Michael has crafted his stories to be sticky and easy to remember. His stories will allow you to understand the SUCCESSFUL StorytellingTM model while the workshop activities will provide prompts to draw out your best ideas so you can develop your own stories.

The Seven Strengths of Successful Problem Solvers

Michael's son helped him realize most people are not taught how to find and solve a problem. In fact, most problem solvers rely on too little information in too short of a time and fall back on bad habits of guessing at a solution leading to unsuccessful projects.

The most successful problem solvers share seven strengths which make them more successful than everyone else. They also use the Einstein Method of Problem Solving to identify the true root cause of a problem, focusing on the 20% of inputs causing 80% of their problems.
